Average Trade Sizes of Top 5 Pools on Terraswap

September 4th 2021

Introduction

Terraswap is the dex feature built into Terra. It enables easy and fast swaps for assets like bLuna that would otherwise require more time. We would like to know what sort of assets are traded on Terraswap the most, and of these, what the average trade size is. This information is useful because it gives us insight into who is exactly making and perhaps dominating the swaps. We can get better segmentation (can determine if we want to continue to serve that audience) or better tailor features to that particular group.

Hypothesis

Before doing any sort of data analysis, I'm going to make a guess that terraswap's top assets are gonna include UST, Luna, and KRT. This is because Luna is the native currency, UST is a core building block, and KRT powers a significant amount of ecommerce in Korea. Knowing this, I expect that for at least the KRT pool, that the average trade size is small since it is dominated by a largely retail userbase. For Luna and UST, I think that the long tail of small trades will get more upwardly skewed by whales than that of KRT. We will seek to validate this hypothesis 1.

Determining the top 5 assets on Terraswap by volume

We can determine the top 5 assets by looking at each swap transaction and breaking it into two parts. We can first sum up the volume of the first swap token and then sum up the volume of the second swap token. We can then union them together and limit our results to get the top 5 assets on Terraswap by volume. Note that this does not reflect Terraswap's actual volume, since we are counting for each asset, effectively double counting total volume.

Here are the top 5 assets by volume.

It appears that the first part of our hypothesis holds up. UST, Luna, and KRT are all among the top 5 assets by volume on Terraswap. They are joined by SDT and MNT, the symbols for stablecoins that peg the IMF's SDR and the Mongolian tögrög.

The SDR is a collection of fiat moneys in an effort to reduce volatility. This makes it attractive as a stablecoin. The tögrög, I honestly have no idea. I would guess that it has some special property or maybe geographic advantage that makes it the fifth most traded.

Determining Average Trade Size of These Assets

Getting the average trade size of these assets is a little more tricky. We can't directly grab the averages since they're split among two different tokens (the token offered and the token taken). What we need to do is grab both the volume and the count for each token, join them by the currency, union them, sum that, and then grab each average (volume / count) grouped by currency 2.

We can see that our hypothesis appears to be correct. UST, Luna, and SDT all have average trade sizes in the range of 13k to 16k USD, whereas KRT is at 5.9k. That is a 2x - 3x scale difference. Although we can not be 100% sure that it is the retail nature of KRT that causes this (we didn't test for this!), the effect appears to be there. We can see that the other stablecoins in the chart are all traded in relatively small quantities, from 0 - 100 whereas MNT's average trade size is 532 USD.

Conclusion

We learned several things here.

  • UST, Luna, KRT, SDR, and MNT are the most traded assets by volume on Terraswap.
  • It is probable that the retail nature of KRT resulted in it's average trade size being less than that of UST, Luna, and SDT.

Appendix

with total_volume_token_1 as (select sum(offer_amount_usd) as amt, token_0_currency as currency from terra.swaps where tx_status = 'SUCCEEDED' group by 2),
total_volume_token_2 as (select sum(offer_amount_usd) as amt, token_1_currency as currency from terra.swaps where tx_status = 'SUCCEEDED' group by 2),
total_count_token_1 as (select token_0_currency as currency, count(1) as cnt from terra.swaps where tx_status = 'SUCCEEDED' group by 1),
total_count_token_2 as (select token_1_currency as currency, count(1) as cnt from terra.swaps where tx_status = 'SUCCEEDED' group by 1)

select amt / cnt as "AVG", currency from (select sum(amt) as amt, sum(cnt) as cnt, currency from (select _ from (select total_count_token_1.currency as currency, amt, cnt from total_volume_token_1
join total_count_token_1
on total_volume_token_1.currency = total_count_token_1.currency)
union all
select * from (select total_count_token_2.currency as currency, amt, cnt from total_volume_token_2
join total_count_token_2
on total_volume_token_2.currency = total_count_token_2.currency)) group by currency);
